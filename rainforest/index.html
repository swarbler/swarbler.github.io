The Deforestation of the Amazon Rainforest

Facts

World's Largest Rainforest

It stretches over 8 countries and covers an area of over 6 million square kilometres!

Rich Biodiversity

The Amazon contains 10% of the world's biodiversity! It is a rich ecosystem of plants, birds, fish, mammals, and insects!

Poor Soil

Most of the nutrients are located in the top soil, which can be washed away by rainfall. The soil underneath appears reddish as it is rich in iron.

Amazon River

The longest river in the world, or second longest if the Nile is longer. It is, however, the largest river in the world by discharge volume of water.

Climate

Hot

Rainy

Humid and Wet

Adaptations of Animals

Poison Dart Frog

The bright colour serves as a warning to predators of its toxicity There are also some other species of frog that aren't poisonous that copy the appearance of poison dart frogs!

Spider Monkey

Its prehensile tail allow the spider monkey to hang and swing from tree branches. The tail can also pick up fruit and throw things!

Jaguar

They are excellent swimmers, adapted to the wet environment of the rainforest. They can move slowly and silently when stalking prey. Their spots also help them blend into the trees.

Sloths

Their grey-brown fur helps them blend into tree trunks, camouflaging them from predators. Their thick, dense coats help them keep dry in the rain. Sloths are very slow because they have a prolonged metabolism, meaning they can stay in the same tree for days before going down to the ground to get food.

Adaptations of Plants

Wide Roots

Trees have thick, strong buttress roots to support them as they grow very tall. Also, as the nutrients are located in the top soil, tree roots grow wide rather than deep.

Drip Tips

The pointed tips of leaves channel water so that the weight of the water does not damage the plant. The waxy coating of the leaves also helps repel rain.

More Photosynthesis!

Leaves under the canopy are much larger to absorb more light, and the stems are flexible to allow the leaves to move with the sun.

Climbing Vines

Epiphytes are plants that grow on the surface of another plant. Lianas (vines) grow around trees as they climb to reach sunlight.

Causes of Deforestation

Commercial Cattle Farming

It causes 80% of the deforestation in the Amazon! As more people consume a westernised diet and demand for beef grows, more land is needed for rearing cattle. Land can't be used for cattle farming for long as cattle eat the top soil, meaning once the soil is depleted of nutrients, they must move and destroy more rainforest.

Commercial Crop Farming

This also requires a lot of land to be deforested, causing 10% of deforestation in the Amazon. The major crop grown is soybeans. Soy isn't just used to tofu and soy milk, it's primarily used to feed livestock such as cattle. Soybean farming in the Amazon has doubled 1990 and 2010.

Settlements

Urbanisation and growth of cities such as Manaus in Brazil is destroying the rainforest. Not only that, other causes of deforestation such as logging or mining require workers. This mean more homes need to be built for them and their families, causing even more deforestation!

Logging

Woods such as mahogany and teak are used for furniture, creating an economic incentive to chop down trees in the rainforest.

Mineral Extraction

Sought after minerals such as gold and silver is found in the Amazon Rainforest. The mines cause deforestation and river pollution.

Government Policies

Policies that promote agriculture and public land allocation can accelerate the rate of deforestation.

Energy Development

Building dams for hydroelectric power and flood the rainforest When submerged forest rots, the water becomes acidic. This can kill fish and other marine life. It also corrodes HEP turbines, making them short-lived. The dam can also be blocked by soil washed down by rain.

Hunting and Poaching

Although not directly causing deforestation, hunting and poaching destabilises the rainforest ecosystem and is prevelant in the Amazon.
