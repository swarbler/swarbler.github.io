Adaptations of Animals

Poison Dart Frog

The bright colour serves as a warning to predators of its toxicity There are also some other species of frog that aren't poisonous that copy the appearance of poison dart frogs!

Spider Monkey

Its prehensile tail allow the spider monkey to hang and swing from tree branches. The tail can also pick up fruit and throw things!

Jaguar

They are excellent swimmers, adapted to the wet environment of the rainforest. They can move slowly and silently when stalking prey. Their spots also help them blend into the trees.

Sloths

Their grey-brown fur helps them blend into tree trunks, camouflaging them from predators. Their thick, dense coats help them keep dry in the rain. Sloths are very slow because they have a prolonged metabolism, meaning they can stay in the same tree for days before going down to the ground to get food.

Adaptations of Plants

Wide Roots

Trees have thick, strong buttress roots to support them as they grow very tall. Also, as the nutrients are located in the top soil, tree roots grow wide rather than deep.

Drip Tips

The pointed tips of leaves channel water so that the weight of the water does not damage the plant. The waxy coating of the leaves also helps repel rain.

More Photosynthesis!

Leaves under the canopy are much larger to absorb more light, and the stems are flexible to allow the leaves to move with the sun.
